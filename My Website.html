What is Electronics?

Electronics is the study and use of electrical devices that control the flow of electrons for functions such as amplification, switching, and power conversion.

Basic Electronic Components

  • Resistor – Limits electrical current.
  • Capacitor – Stores electrical energy temporarily.
  • Inductor – Stores energy in a magnetic field.
  • Diode – Allows one-direction current flow.
  • Transistor – Acts as a switch/amplifier.
  • LED – Emits light when powered.
  • Breadboard – Used for building circuits without soldering.

Ohm's Law

One of the most important laws in electronics:

V = I × R

• V = Voltage (Volts)
• I = Current (Amps)
• R = Resistance (Ohms)

Simple Circuit Example

A basic circuit includes a power source, resistor, and LED:

  • Battery → Resistor → LED → Back to Battery

Safety Tips

  • Never touch live wires.
  • Use proper insulation.
  • Avoid short circuits.
  • Verify polarity before powering circuits.
